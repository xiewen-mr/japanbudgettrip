The best Japan travel insurance is the policy that protects the parts of your trip that would actually hurt to lose: emergency medical care, nonrefundable flights and hotels, missed connections, delayed baggage, and support when language or paperwork becomes difficult.

Quick answerMost tourists should compare medical coverage first, then cancellation and interruption rules, baggage limits, delay benefits, 24/7 assistance, and exclusions for the exact activities on the itinerary.

Start with medical coverage

Medical coverage is the first filter. Check the maximum limit, emergency treatment, hospitalization, outpatient care, medical evacuation, pre-existing condition rules, and whether the assistance team can coordinate with providers.

Then compare trip protection

Cancellation and interruption benefits matter most when flights, hotels, tours, ski packages, or theme park tickets are nonrefundable. Do not just look for the word cancellation; read the covered reasons and required documents.

CancellationBefore departure

Check illness, family emergencies, airline disruption, natural events, and whether a doctor's note or proof is required.

InterruptionAfter the trip starts

Look for unused bookings, extra transport, and early-return costs if the itinerary breaks.

DelayLate flights and missed plans

Check the minimum delay hours, daily cap, covered expenses, and whether receipts are required.

BaggageLost or delayed bags

Look for total limits, per-item limits, electronics caps, and carrier report requirements.

Is credit card insurance enough?

Credit card travel insurance may be useful, but it is not automatically enough. Confirm whether every traveler is covered, whether the booking must be paid with that card, medical limits, cancellation limits, baggage caps, and emergency contact procedures.

Exclusions that matter in Japan

  • Skiing, snowboarding, cycling, hiking, diving, or rental car activities.
  • Pre-existing medical conditions or medication-related issues.
  • High-value electronics above per-item limits.
  • Claims without receipts, medical notes, police reports, or carrier reports.
  • Weather, strikes, or disruptions that are not listed as covered reasons.

Safe buying order

  1. List nonrefundable flights, hotels, tours, and tickets.
  2. Check your health needs, medications, and planned activities.
  3. Compare medical limits and emergency assistance first.
  4. Compare cancellation, interruption, baggage, and delay benefits.
  5. Save policy number, emergency phone number, and claim instructions offline.

FAQ

Should I buy Japan travel insurance before booking hotels?

It is smart to compare coverage before committing to nonrefundable bookings. Some cancellation benefits can depend on when you buy the policy relative to your first trip payment.

Does Japan travel insurance cover lost luggage?

Many policies include baggage benefits, but limits and documents vary. Check per-item caps, electronics rules, delayed baggage terms, and whether you need a carrier or police report.

Does travel insurance cover Japan earthquakes or typhoons?

Coverage depends on the policy wording, timing, and the event. Read natural disaster, trip interruption, delay, and cancellation rules before relying on coverage.
